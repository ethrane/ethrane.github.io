Photos from Super-K


Super-Kamiokande is a neutrino detector located in Japan near the city of Toyama, which is west-northwest of Tokyo. It is cylindrical in shape and contains 50,000 tons of ultrapurified water. In order to shield it from cosmic radiation, the detector, which is roughly the height of a thirteen story building, is located under 1 km of rock in Mt. Ikeno. Relativistic particles, produced from neutrino interactions in and around the detector, produce Cherenkov radiation as they travel through the detector, and this light is detected by the detector's ~11,000 40 cm photomultiplier tubes (PMTs).
